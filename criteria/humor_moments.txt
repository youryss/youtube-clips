# Humor Moments Criteria

Identify genuinely funny or entertaining segments that are shareable.

Types of humor to look for:
- Self-deprecating humor
- Relatable everyday situations
- Unexpected twists or punchlines
- Witty observations
- Playful sarcasm
- Amusing analogies or metaphors
- Funny storytelling
- Comedic timing

Indicators of humor:
- Jokes or puns
- Funny anecdotes
- Absurd or exaggerated scenarios
- Ironic observations
- Pop culture references
- Relatable frustrations delivered humorously
- Unexpected comparisons
- Comic relief moments

Examples:
- Funny personal stories
- Relatable fails or mishaps
- Amusing industry inside jokes
- Creative metaphors
- Satirical takes on common situations
- Light-hearted roasting
- Playful banter

Look for segments that:
- Make you smile or laugh
- Are quotable or shareable
- Have good comedic timing
- Don't rely on mean-spirited humor
- Are appropriate for broad audiences
- Add entertainment value while maintaining message
- Use humor to make a point

Score segments higher if they:
- Are genuinely funny (not forced)
- Are relatable to the target audience
- Add to rather than distract from the content
- Are self-contained (don't need context)
- Have rewatchability
- Balance humor with value
